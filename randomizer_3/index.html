お みくじ

[omikuji]

Omikuji are random fortunes written on strips of paper at Shinto shrines and Buddhist temples in Japan. Literally "sacred lot", these are usually received by making a small offering (generally a five-yen coin as it is considered good luck) and randomly choosing one from a box, hoping for the resulting fortune to be good. Another method would be to shake an omikuji box until a wooden stick with a number label (omikuji hashi) falls out. The number would then correspond
to a specific fortune slip.
